School Classrooms

This is what our Classrooms Look Like

Daves Creek Elementary School is a public school located in Cumming, GA, which is in a large suburb setting. The student population of Daves Creek Elementary School is 1,217, and the school serves PK-5. At Daves Creek Elementary School, 94% of students scored at or above the proficient level for math, and 92% scored at or above that level for reading. The school’s minority student enrollment is 87%. The student-teacher ratio is 16:1, which is the same as that of the district. The student population is made up of 47% female students and 53% male students. The school enrolls 3% economically disadvantaged students. There are 77 equivalent full-time teachers and 2 full-time school counselors.

Green Squad

This is what Green squad Looks Like

The mission of Daves Creek Elementary is developing children's excellence and success (DCES). This includes promoting environmental education through a school-wide recycling program, various environmental events, and an after school environmental education program for 4th and 5th grade students, the Daves Creek Green Team. School programs are designed to raise awareness of the problems associated with waste disposal and to empower students, parents and the community to reduce waste through recycling. To date, Daves Creek has kept more than 50 tons of material from the landfill. Recycling ROCKS!

Art Class

This is what Art class Look Like

Art is a nine-week connections program that involves the process of creating, connecting, presenting and responding to art based upon the Georgia Standards of Excellence for Visual Arts. With freedom to choose materials and subject matter, students engage in personalized learning and discover new strengths along the way. Students learn about art and creativity, explore with materials and techniques, understand the functions and foundations, and make meaningful connections through art. When students focus on the Studio Habits of Mind and the FCS Learner Profile, we develop the whole artist. Students will understand that art is a journey and that it takes practice and persistence to succeed.
